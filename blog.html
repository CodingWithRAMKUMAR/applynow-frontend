How to Get Your First IT Job in 2026: A Complete Guide
📅 April 10, 2026 | ✍️ Ram Kumar

The IT job market in India is competitive but full of opportunities. Here’s a step‑by‑step roadmap for freshers:

1. Build 2‑3 Solid Projects

Your projects are your experience. Build a full‑stack web app, a data analysis dashboard, or a mobile app. Host them on GitHub and deploy on free platforms like Vercel or Render. Employers want to see what you can actually build.

2. Contribute to Open Source

Open source contributions show you can work in a team, read others’ code, and follow best practices. Start with “good first issue” labels on GitHub.

3. Network on LinkedIn (the right way)

Don’t just send connection requests. Post about your projects, comment on recruiters’ posts, and share what you’re learning. Recruiters notice active, genuine profiles.

4. Apply Strategically

Use ApplyMore to find only 0‑2 year jobs. Customise your resume for each role – match the keywords from the job description. Quality over quantity.

5. Prepare for Technical Interviews

Practice DSA problems on LeetCode (easy/medium), SQL queries, and be ready to explain your projects. Mock interviews help reduce anxiety.

Remember, every rejection is a step closer to the right offer. Keep improving, keep applying.

Top 10 In‑Demand Skills for IT Freshers in 2026
📅 March 30, 2026 | ✍️ Ram Kumar

Based on thousands of job postings on ApplyMore, these are the skills companies are actively hiring for:

  1. Python – Data analysis, backend, automation.
  2. SQL – Every company uses databases.
  3. Cloud Basics (AWS/Azure) – EC2, S3, serverless.
  4. Git & GitHub – Version control is mandatory.
  5. JavaScript / TypeScript – Frontend and backend (Node.js).
  6. Data Structures & Algorithms – Required for coding interviews.
  7. React or Angular – Popular frontend frameworks.
  8. REST APIs – Understanding how to build and consume APIs.
  9. Basic Linux – Command line, shell scripting.
  10. AI/ML Fundamentals – Even basic knowledge of LLMs and prompt engineering is a plus.

Pick 2‑3 skills to focus on first. Build a small project that combines them. Then move to the next.

Resume Tips for IT Freshers: What Recruiters Look For
📅 March 15, 2026 | ✍️ Ram Kumar

Your resume has about 6 seconds to impress a recruiter. Here’s how to make it count:

  • Keep it to one page – Freshers don’t need more.
  • Highlight projects – For each project, write what you built, the tech stack, and a measurable result (e.g., “Reduced load time by 30%”).
  • List technical skills prominently – Programming languages, frameworks, tools.
  • Include internships (even virtual) – Describe your contributions.
  • Add GitHub and LinkedIn links – Make sure they are active and professional.
  • Avoid clichés – “Hardworking”, “team player” – instead, show examples.
  • Use a clean, ATS‑friendly format – No tables, no fancy graphics. Use standard sections: Education, Skills, Projects, Internships, Certifications.

Tailor your resume for each job. Match keywords from the job description. Save different versions for different roles (Java, Python, Data Analyst).

Remote vs. Onsite Jobs: Which is Better for Freshers?
📅 March 10, 2026 | ✍️ Ram Kumar

Both remote and onsite jobs have pros and cons for freshers. Here’s a breakdown:

Remote Jobs – Pros

  • Flexibility – work from anywhere.
  • No commute – save time and money.
  • Access to companies across India (not limited to your city).

Remote Jobs – Cons

  • Less mentorship – harder to ask quick questions.
  • Requires self‑discipline and good communication.
  • May feel isolated.

Onsite Jobs – Pros

  • Better mentorship – learn by watching seniors.
  • Build stronger relationships with colleagues.
  • Separate work and personal life.

Onsite Jobs – Cons

  • Commute and relocation costs.
  • Less flexibility.

For freshers, onsite or hybrid roles often provide better learning opportunities. But if you are self‑motivated and have a good home setup, remote can work too. ApplyMore lists both – use the location filter to find onsite roles in your city.

How to Negotiate Your First Salary as a Fresher
📅 April 16, 2026 | ✍️ Ram Kumar

Many freshers hesitate to negotiate, but it’s normal and expected. Here’s how:

  • Research market rates – Use Glassdoor, AmbitionBox, or AskApplyMore (coming soon).
  • Know your value – Highlight projects, internships, and unique skills.
  • Be polite but confident – “Based on my research and skills, I was hoping for a range of X‑Y.”
  • Consider total package – Benefits, learning opportunities, and work culture matter too.
  • Don’t demand – Negotiate, don’t ultimatum.

Even a small increase in starting salary compounds over your career. Practice with a friend before the call.

How to Prepare for a Java Developer Interview as a Fresher
📅 April 15, 2026 | ✍️ Ram Kumar

Java remains one of the most sought‑after skills for fresher IT jobs. Here’s how to prepare effectively:

  1. Master core Java concepts – OOP, collections, multithreading, exception handling.
  2. Practice coding problems – Use platforms like LeetCode or HackerRank (easy/medium level).
  3. Understand basic SQL – Joins, group by, subqueries.
  4. Build a small project – A simple CRUD application using Spring Boot and MySQL.
  5. Prepare for HR questions – “Tell me about yourself”, “Why do you want to work here?”

Remember, interviewers value problem‑solving approach over perfect syntax. Explain your thought process clearly.
